How to Write a Website Requirement Document (WRD)

Before you hire a developer on Romankind, you need a plan. A Website Requirement Document (WRD) ensures your developer understands your vision, saving you time and money.

Step 1: Define Your Business Goals

Every great website starts with a "Why." Are you selling products? Building a personal brand? Or creating a lead generation tool? Write down your primary goal. For example: "I want to get 10 new customer inquiries per week via a contact form."

Step 2: List Your Essential Features

Don't just say "I want a website." Be specific about functionality:

  • Responsive Design: Must work perfectly on mobile and desktop.
  • Contact Forms: For users to send details directly to your email.
  • Blog/Resources: To share updates and improve SEO.
  • E-commerce: If you plan to accept payments.
Pro Tip: At Romankind, our request form is designed to capture these details automatically for you. Click here to submit your requirements.

Step 3: Visual Style and Brand Identity

Developers aren't mind readers. Provide examples of websites you like. Mention your preferred color schemes (e.g., "Professional Blue and White") and whether you have a logo ready. This helps the developer calculate the design effort needed.

Step 4: Technical Preferences

If you prefer a specific technology like Next.js, WordPress, or React, mention it. If you aren't sure, ask the developer for a recommendation based on your budget and speed requirements.

Conclusion: A clear document is the foundation of a successful website. Use these steps to prepare before reaching out to our experts.
